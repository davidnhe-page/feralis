Feralis Project

The Heart of My Work

The Feralis Project was born from the need to create deeper and more authentic communication bridges between humans and horses. Through years of observation, study, and coexistence with these animals, I have developed methodologies that respect the essence and nature of the horse.

This project is based on three fundamental pillars: education through workshops and seminars, dissemination through publications and digital content, and the creation of a community of horse lovers who share values of respect and mutual understanding.

Your economic support allows us to maintain and expand this project, reaching more people and horses who can benefit from these teachings.

About David Castro

Writer and Researcher. Specialist in Human-Horse Relationships.

From Training to Liberation - A Life with Horses. With over two decades of work alongside horses, David Castro has traveled a path of profound transformation that took him from being a renowned horse trainer to an international reference in respectful education and animal rights activism.

His journey began more than 20 years ago, where for a decade he stood out as a trainer, known for his ability and speed in achieving exceptional results with non-violent techniques. However, his search for a more authentic relationship led him to transcend the concept of "training" to embrace one of "education."

This paradigm shift led him to found the Space with Horses (2010) and, after being appointed representative of the Nevzorov Haute Ecole in Argentina (2012), he founded the first Argentine School of Hippology (2013). His influence grew with the publication of his two books, "The Silence of Horses" (2015) and "And I Whispered to the Horse" (2018), and dozens of articles that denounce equine exploitation and promote respect.

Since 2017, his work has expanded beyond horses with the "Feralis Project," a research initiative for coexistence between humans and all dependent animals (cows, pigs, dogs, chickens, etc.), exploring rewilding and life in shared territories with harmony.

For over twelve years, David has traveled self-managed through America and Europe, giving talks, workshops, and supporting sanctuaries. His mission today is clear: to educate to understand, respect, and liberate, working for a future where the relationship with other animals is based on respect and not exploitation.
